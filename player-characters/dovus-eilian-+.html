This is Dovus Eilian, in the year 711. Artificer by trade, connected to the Essurian Engineer's Guild. Whoever's listening, please, don't make the Doofus joke in your head... it's gotten old, over the years. My parents really did a number on me with that, all because they saw a white dove fly up towards the moonlit sky, just before their first kiss... sigh By Syldara, do I miss them... 

Anyway. As said, I'm Dovus. This is... a timepiece, as I call it. It's a reflection on my life, from the vantage point of the ripe age of 75. A good time to reflect on a lifetime of adventure and wonder, or so I thought. Let's start from the beginning. 

I was born in Essuria. As a kid, I was always fascinated with birds, gazing at them as they soared high in the sky. I distinctly remember, at the age of five, thinking to myself: I want to fly just like them. Upon telling this to my mother, she said that, with hard work, perhaps I could, one day. I looked at her, astonished. How?! Then, she told me about a place that has set the course for all my life after it: the Engineer's Guild. As she explained this marvellous place, rife with experiments and inventions, I decided that it was there that I needed to be. Of course, the Guild has no use for five year-olds, so I had to wait. But I did not idle. Every spare second thereafter, I used in order to prepare myself for that illustrious future of learning. I read, and read, and read, and tinkered, and tinkered, and tinkered. I think at least a hundred iterations passed before I finally completed my first, fully functioning flying contraption: a dove, which I aptly named Columbo. It has been my companion ever since, though naught a bolt remains the same as that dear initial version of my mechanical avian. 

As it turned out, the Guild was rather impressed with Columbo, and decided to allow me to become an apprentice. Years of hard work ensued, in which I honed my skills and perfected my craft. I studied under the great Nyla Sonore, the most brilliant woman I have ever met. Even to this day, I have not even come close to her level of craftsmanship. She taught me all I know, and more. I remember those years fondly... 

Although, as everything must, those times, too, came to pass. It was then that Mammon Avari published a call for brave adventurers to join him on his quest into the wilds. Intrigued, I signed up, though with little hope of being accepted for such an arduous and dangerous journey. Little did I know of what was to come... 

With a heavy heart, I said my goodbyes to my loving parents, Arial and Elna, and set out with a strange band of companions, who I will introduce shortly. Before that however, dear listener, a warning. What I am about to tell you may well frighten you to the core. For the secrets we found... they rocked my world to its core, and I have never been the same. You have been warned…

First, there was Lucy Nightglow +. A young girl with druidic powers, she was a real sweetheart. Sadly, before we set out on our journey, the city was attackd by a band of greenskins, in which her father valiantly sacrificed himself to save her life. This great loss caused her untold grief, and upon witnessing her father's swift burial, I myself, too, broke down in tears. Only now can I understand how this proved to be a portent of things to come… 

Then, there was Kemo Kantaki +. A reserved man, quiet, and careful with his words. He was no soft city-dweller, but rather a rugged outdoorsman, used to sleeping rough and braving the dangers of the outside. 

Finally, we were joined by Sumon Takh, a Firbolg and librarian in Essuria, whose library had burned down in the greenskin raid. A gentle giant, he disliked [Mammon](Mammon Avari) as much as the rest of us, it seemed, but decided that the city held nothing more for him.

Thus, we set out on our quest. First, we headed to a small village, braving swamp and forest. I fell sick due to some horrid leech, but recovered soon enough. There, we encountered Lucy's grandma, as well as Kemo's father! While Lucy was overjoyed to see her beloved elder, Kemo was quite the opposite. He seemed even more dour than usual… 

Sumon decided to leave us, and we set out towards our objective: a place called The Pit. Now, if that name already sounds ominous, the place itself was much, much worse. A massive hole in the ground, with some sort of inverse tower in the middle. We could barely even see the bottom… we were greeted by some utterly soulless person, Morose, and had to solve some riddles to enter into the place proper. Inside, we were greeted by… Bob. A monster unlike any I had ever seen, or have in all my years thereafter. It had an insane twinkle in each of its eyes, and seemed not just a little cruel. From here, we could see that inside The Pit, the people working were half-rotting creatures, and as Bob gleefully explained, he was mining them… for their hope. I am proud to say, that after many long years, I finally managed to destroy Bob and his tower for once and for all. But that is a story for later… 

As first, we headed into the crypts. What we found there, dear listener, may well shake you to your core…

Wait, you're still listening? Well, then, let me share with you a little secret: I am actually only 28 years old, not 75… this 'timepiece' was to be my retrospective, but I must admit that, having arrived at our destination, I have a gnawing fear that I may not reach that ripe old age intact… I'm lying in bed in some sort of magical room, created by that terrifying creature with many eyes and an insane look in each of them. Tomorrow morning, we set out into the crypts… and I do not know what we will face there. I hope everything will turn out alright… for now, though, dear listener, good night, and good luck.

Death

Dovus Eilian died on April 17th, 664, at the age of 28, by the hand of the Subterranean Shambler, attempting to retrieve the Shard of Earth for Mammon Avari.
